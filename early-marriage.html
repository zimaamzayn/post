Similarly, in the Sasanian Empire, Zoroastrian jurisprudence permitted guardians to contract marriages for minors, but emphasized that sexual relations should occur only after the girl reached puberty. According to Maria Macuch’s analysis of the Pahlavi legal text Mādigān ī Hazār Dādestān (Book of a Thousand Judgments), the wife’s consent upon reaching maturity was increasingly recognized, and puberty (baromān) was typically defined as age 9 or 12 for girls in religious legal discourse (Macuch, Rechtskasuistik und Gerichtspraxis zu Sasanidischer Zeit, Harrassowitz Verlag, 2008, pp. 142–145).
