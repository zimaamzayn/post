Balancing Faith and Medicine

By Zimaam Zayn

Introduction

AoA, for me life is about keeping faith and studies together. As a medical student at MRIMS, my days are a mix of learning, faith, and sometimes thinking deeply about life.

Act 1: The Beginning

My name is Zimaam Zayn, and I study medicine at MRIMS. I’m not the smartest student, but I like writing notes and going back to them before exams. These notes always help me pass. I spend most of my time with a few close friends who keep me happy and relaxed.

I am usually with my pc and books, but sometimes I become talkative with friends. I make plans for goals, but I often complete them at the last moment or miss them.

Act 2: The Struggle

It’s hard to balance medical studies with my love for screenwriting and reading books. Writing and reading give me joy, but I often doubt myself. Still, I feel alive when I write or read something new.

I also like reading books, listening to Noman Ali Khan’s Tafseer, and watching good dramas. These things give me peace or help me pass time. I don’t use my phone for more than 30 minutes in a day.

Act 3: The Harmony

Through all this, I keep learning about myself. I have grown with time and even learned from people who said I was useless.

Climax: The Breakthrough

One big moment for me was when I managed to prepare for exams and also finish a screenwriting and reading books project alone. That success gave me confidence that I can handle both studies and writing together. Isn’t that what we all want?

Resolution: The Final End

To my fellow medicos: life is not easy to balance, but it is worth it. Try, learn, and stay true to your faith. Even if you can’t spread positivity, at least don’t lose it yourself.

"Life is a Balance between Holding on and Letting Go." ~ Rumi

The future holds many possibilities, and I am excited to explore them, guided by my faith and fueled by my passion for medicine, reading, and screenwriting. Allah-hafiz...
